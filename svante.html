Temperature-Controlled Test Rig for Carbon Capture

Test apparatus for evaluating carbon capture material performance under controlled temperature conditions.

Role: Mechanical and thermal design, instrumentation
Tools: CAD, basic thermal analysis, Arduino / DAQ

Overview

This project involved designing and building a bench-scale test rig to evaluate a carbon capture process under controlled temperature conditions. The system used thermoelectric elements, heat spreading, insulation, and instrumentation to achieve stable plate temperatures and log data during experiments.

I was responsible for the mechanical layout of the temperature-control assembly, integration of sensors, and practical considerations around manufacturability, wiring, and repeatable setup.

Responsibilities & Contributions

  • Designed the temperature-control plate stack-up, including heat spreader and insulation.
  • Integrated thermoelectric modules (TECs), heat sinks, and mechanical fastening strategy.
  • Selected and placed temperature sensors and associated instrumentation points.
  • Collaborated on control strategy and integration with the electronics/firmware team.
  • Created mechanical drawings and assembly instructions for fabrication and setup.
  • Assisted in initial testing and troubleshooting of system behaviour and temperature response.

Engineering Requirements

Key requirements for the temperature-controlled test rig included:

  • Provide a controlled, uniform plate temperature across the test area.
  • Reach and maintain specified setpoints within an acceptable tolerance band.
  • Accommodate the physical test sample geometry and mounting method.
  • Allow integration of multiple temperature sensors for monitoring and logging.
  • Remain compact and practical for lab use, with accessible wiring and components.
  • Be robust enough for repeated use and reconfiguration during development.

Design & Integration

The temperature-control plate was structured as a stack-up of a heat spreader, thermoelectric modules, and a heat sink, clamped together with a consistent preload. I designed the plate geometry and mounting features to ensure good thermal contact while keeping the assembly manufacturable with available tooling.

Cut-outs and routing features were added for cable management and sensor placement. I worked with the electronics team to ensure adequate provisions for wiring, connectors, and clearance around the test area.

Assembly / plate layout

Testing & Outcome

Initial testing verified that the assembly could reach target temperatures and maintain them within the specified band for extended periods. The rig provided a repeatable platform for evaluating carbon capture samples under different thermal conditions.

The project demonstrated my ability to combine mechanical design, basic thermal considerations, and practical lab-level integration, while documenting the system so that other team members could operate and adapt it.
