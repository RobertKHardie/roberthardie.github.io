IndyCar Electric Conversion – Structural Motor Mount

Custom load-bearing motor mount enabling electric powertrain integration into a Reynard IndyCar chassis.

Role: Mechanical design and analysis
Tools: SolidWorks, FEA, technical documentation

Overview

This project involved converting a vintage Reynard IndyCar chassis to an electric powertrain. My primary responsibility was the design and analysis of the structural motor mount and load-bearing member that supports the electric motor and ties it into the existing transaxle and chassis.

The assembly had to maintain alignment between the motor and transaxle, handle torque and bump-related loading, and remain feasible to manufacture within the capabilities of a student team working with limited machining resources.

Responsibilities & Contributions

  • Led structural design of the motor mount and load-bearing member.
  • Developed and iterated CAD models for packaging, stiffness, and manufacturability.
  • Performed FEA on key load cases (drive torque, vertical loads, and bump/shock scenarios).
  • Established critical dimensions and tolerances for motor–transaxle alignment.
  • Produced detailed drawings and documentation for fabrication.
  • Participated in fabrication checks and fit-up with the existing chassis and transaxle.
  • Chaired team meetings related to the mechanical integration scope and maintained design documentation.

Engineering Requirements

Key requirements for the motor mount and structural member included:

  • Maintain motor–transaxle shaft alignment within defined tolerances under load.
  • Support torque loads from the electric motor and driveline.
  • Withstand bump and shock loads transmitted through the chassis.
  • Fit within existing chassis geometry and mounting points without invasive modifications.
  • Be manufacturable with available machining and fabrication resources.
  • Allow reasonable access for assembly and maintenance of adjacent components.

Design & Analysis

I began with a survey of the existing chassis hardpoints and transaxle mounting surfaces, then developed several conceptual layouts for the motor mount and supporting structure. After selecting a concept, I modelled the full assembly in CAD, focusing on load paths, stiffness, and manufacturability.

FEA was used to evaluate stress and deflection under multiple load cases, including drive torque and vertical loads derived from vehicle mass and acceleration conditions. Iterations focused on:

  • Reducing stress concentrations at transitions and weld regions.
  • Improving stiffness to limit misalignment under load.
  • Simplifying machining and weld access where possible.
CAD / FEA render

Fabrication & Outcome

The final design was released with detailed drawings, including critical datums and tolerances. Components were machined and welded according to the drawings, then trial-fit to the chassis and transaxle.

The assembly met the packaging and alignment requirements and provided a robust structure for the electric motor installation. This work contributed to a major milestone in the overall IndyCar electric conversion and established a foundation for future system integration and testing.
